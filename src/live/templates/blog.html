Understanding the Signal Card

Each card on the AI Signal Engine dashboard represents a single stock from your watchlist. Here's what each section means:

  • AI Score (0‚Äì100): The weighted aggregate of 8+ technical indicators. Higher = stronger conviction.
  • Signal Type: STRONG BUY, BUY, NEUTRAL, SELL, STRONG SELL ‚Äî derived from the score.
  • Entry Price: The recommended price level to enter the trade.
  • Stop Loss: Your exit point if the trade goes against you. Always respect this!
  • Targets 1 & 2: Profit-taking levels. Book partial profits at T1, trail at T2.

Translating Signals to Options Trades

When you see BULLISH ‚Üí Buy CE, it means:

  • Open the Option Chain modal (click the ‚õìÔ∏è button)
  • Look for the ATM (At-The-Money) strike ‚Äî highlighted in the table
  • Buy the Call Option (CE) at that strike for the nearest expiry
  • Set your stop loss based on the premium, not just the underlying
üí° Pro Tip: Check the OI PCR pill on the card. If PCR > 1.0 and the signal is bullish, there's strong put-writing support. This confirms the trend!

The Option Chain Modal

Clicking ‚õìÔ∏è Option Chain on any signal card opens a theoretical pricing model powered by Black-Scholes. Here's how to read it:

  • Strikes: The price levels at which you can buy/sell the option. ATM is closest to the current market price.
  • Delta: How much the option price moves per ‚Çπ1 move in the stock. 0.5 delta = ‚Çπ0.50 change per ‚Çπ1 stock move.
  • IV (Implied Volatility): Higher IV = costlier premiums. Avoid buying when IV is spiked.

Strike Selection Rules

  • For directional trades: Buy ATM or 1-strike ITM. Higher delta = more responsive.
  • For scalping: Buy ATM with tight SL. Exit at 10-20% premium gain.
  • For swing: Go 1-2 strikes OTM if the score is >80. More leverage but more risk.
üí° Pro Tip: Compare the theoretical price shown in our chain with the actual market price on your broker. If the market premium is much higher, IV is elevated ‚Äî consider selling rather than buying.

What is PCR (Put-Call Ratio)?

The Put-Call Ratio is calculated as Total Put OI / Total Call OI. It tells us where the "smart money" is positioned:

  • PCR > 1.0: More puts being written than calls ‚Üí Market makers expect prices to rise (Bullish)
  • PCR < 0.7: More calls being written ‚Üí Market makers expect prices to fall or stay flat (Bearish)
  • PCR 0.7‚Äì1.0: Mixed/neutral ‚Äî no strong directional bias from options market

How Our Engine Uses PCR

The AI Signal Engine adds PCR as a 10% weight in its scoring model. But more importantly, it generates Fakeout Warnings:

  • If technicals say BULLISH but PCR < 0.8 ‚Üí ‚ö†Ô∏è Fakeout Risk: Options OI is Bearish
  • If technicals say BEARISH but PCR > 1.1 ‚Üí ‚ö†Ô∏è Fakeout Risk: Options OI is Bullish
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: PCR alone is not a trading signal. Always combine it with the full AI score, price action, and your own analysis. Never risk more than 2% of your capital on a single trade.

Practical Example

Suppose CANBK shows Score: 85 | STRONG BUY | PCR: 1.35. This is a high-confluence setup because:

  • Technical indicators are strongly bullish (85/100)
  • Options market confirms with heavy put writing (PCR 1.35)
  • You can confidently buy the ATM Call with defined risk
